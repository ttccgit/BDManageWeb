Does my job affect my metabolism?

Article By: William Sukala, MS, CSCS

Q: Does sitting still at a desk for a long period affect my metabolism?

A: Technically, sitting at a desk does not slow down your metabolism any more than sitting at home watching TV all day. If that's all you do, then yes, it will slow down your metabolism. Physical inactivity causes your muscles to atrophy (shrink). As a result, they burn fewer calories, thus facilitating body-fat accumulation.

Even though work is a necessary evil to keep the bill collectors away, it's what you do in your off-time that really counts. You should maximize exercise time before or after work, or during your lunch break. For example, bring your workout clothes to the office. Try to log a half hour of walking time on your lunch break. This way, you eat less and you maximize calorie expenditure. Get out from behind that desk. Get up and move around during the day to stave off muscle stiffness.
