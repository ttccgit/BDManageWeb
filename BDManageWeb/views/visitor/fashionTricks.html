How to look leaner when you´re on your way to skinny jeans.

Article By: Camille Noe

Want to appear long and lean? Look no farther than your own closet! We talked to two top fashion experts to get the scoop on how to dress your best–even as you´re working toward knocking off those last pounds.

1. Think color
Dressing slim doesn´t necessarily mean wearing all black. "That can be boring, and may even make your complexion seem dull. You´re better off wearing colors that flatter you," says Alison Deyette, a former fashion editor.

"The trick is to dress in one shade from head to toe – a teal top with a pair of blue jeans, for example – which presents your body as a long, unbroken line." If you do mix colors, be sure to wear the lighter hue on top and the darker on the bottom, which will draw attention to your face – and pull it away from your hips and thighs.

2. Focus on your overall body shape, not your "trouble spots."
"Worrying about your ´big butt´ while you´re getting dressed is a recipe for disaster – you´ll end up with an outfit that hides your behind but does nothing for your best features," says Kendall Farr, a fashion stylist and author of The Pocket Stylist (Gotham/Penguin, 2004). Obsessing about one dreaded body part can also lead to self-loathing, adds Farr. Instead, she suggests looking in the mirror and pretending that you can only see a silhouette. How wide are your shoulders in relation to your hips? Are you more apple or pear shaped? Take note and wear clothes that balance your entire body.

3. Wear clothes that really fit.
"The number one style sin is wearing clothes that are a size too small, which only accentuates flaws," says Deyette. "You may feel better about yourself thinking you´re an 8 rather than a 10, but who´s going to see the tag on your pants? It´s better to buy up and not look like you poured yourself into your outfit. " Conversely, oversized duds are a no-no, too. "They´ll only make you look bigger, says Farr. "Look for clothes that show off – not hide – your shape."

4. Make your accessories work for you.
"Big, bold accessories can make you appear smaller and can draw attention to your favorite features," says Deyette. Highlight your hands or face by donning a cocktail ring or a gorgeous pair of chandelier earrings. Other accessories can add the illusion of length; a scarf around the neck or a long necklace can make your upper body look leaner.

5. If all else fails, remember these tried-and-true basics:
V-neck tops are universally flattering, as they make the neck look longer, says Deyette. Similarly, an A-line skirt that stops just below the knee looks terrific on any body shape. Opt for single-breasted jackets rather than double-breasted; the extra material won´t do you any favors. And toss the pleated pants: "The pleats draw attention to the stomach and thighs," says Deyette. Finally, of course, if you´re not on your feet all day long, there´s nothing to lengthen your legs – and boost your confidence – like a great pair of heels.
