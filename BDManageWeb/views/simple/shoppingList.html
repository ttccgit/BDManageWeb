Simple Start Food List

This list contains ingredients for all of the Simple Start meals and snacks: these are Power Foods (plus some other oils, seasonings and condiments, which are OK to eat). Think of this as a shopping list: Stock your fridge, freezer and pantry with the foods that appeal to you most!

Fat-free dairy & dairy substitutes

Go for it!

  • Cheese, fat-free (including cottage cheese) and unflavored fat-free soy cheese
  • Milk, fat-free/non-fat/skim, soy (unflavored) and beverages made with these milks such as cappuccino or latte (as long as they're sugar-free)
  • Yogurt, fat-free (artificial sweetener only) and unflavored soy
  • Fat-free sour cream

Skip it:

  • Low-fat dairy (it's not fat-free!)

Fruit

Go for it!

  • All fresh, frozen, or canned fruit without added sugar
  • Fruit canned in its own juice (drained)
  • Fruit salad-mixed fruit with no added sugar

Skip it:

  • Dried fruit
  • Juices

Vegetables

Go for it!

  • Most fresh, frozen or canned without sugar or oil

Skip it:

  • Vegetable juices
  • Pickled vegetables
  • Avocados
  • French fries
  • Olives
  • Plantains

Healthy oils (limit to 2 tsp per day)

Go for it!

  • Olive
  • Canola
  • Safflower
  • Sunflower
  • Flaxseed

Lean proteins

Go for it!

  • Beans and peas, dried or canned
  • Meats or fish without breading or added fat
  • Lentils
  • All lean meats, trimmed, skin removed
  • Eggs: whole, whites and egg substitute
  • Meat substitutes (including tofu and veggie burgers with 2g fat or less)
  • Most fish and shellfish
  • Organ meats: beef, lamb, pork or veal

Skip it:

  • Canned fish or shellfish packed in oil
  • Processed meats, such as hot dogs

Whole grains

Go for it!

  • Brown and wild rice
  • Hot cereals, without added sugar, dried fruits, or nuts
  • Pasta, whole wheat or whole grain
  • Popcorn-air-popped, plain or light microwave popcorn
  • Cold cereal, whole-grain, 4g fiber or more per serving (no nuts, dried fruit, or added sugar)
  • Whole grains such as quinoa and barley

Plus more

Go for it!

  • Light or reduced-calorie bread (whole grains are the best choice)
  • Broth, onion and some broth-and-tomato-based vegetable soups
  • Sugar-free gelatin
  • Potatoes-white, red, sweet

Seasonings & condiments

Go for it!

  • Fat-free dressings, salsas and mayo
  • Extracts, spices, sugar substitutes and fresh and dried herbs
  • Sauces: Teriyaki, steak, mustard, soy, taco, cocktail, Worcestershire, ketchup, hot sauce
  • Nonstick cooking spray, baking powder, baking soda
  • Vinegar, capers
  • Fat-free margarine
