Eating right and exercising are the major ways to achieve your goals. But your makeup bag holds the secret to a few sculpting tricks as well.

Article By: Sheryl Kraft

The whole point of makeup is to enhance your natural beauty, so it makes sense that makeup artists have taken that a step further, using makeup to create the illusion of prominent cheekbones, a narrower jawline, a fuller mouth. We asked two New York City makeup artists to open their makeup bag of tricks and share their real-life techniques for how to slim a face, instantly.

Arch Your Brows

"There are many tricks for making the face appear slimmer," says eyebrow expert Amy Kernahan. Eyebrows can make an especially big impact. Here are some easy-to-follow tips for shaping yours.

Rule #1: Full brows, slim face. "A skinny brow can make a face appear too full," explains Kernahan. Pluck only strays, following the natural curve of your brow. (And never pluck above the browline; if you can´t resist, at least leave it to the professionals). Kernahan recommends using tweezers with a slanted tip for easier hair removal. Tweezerman is one of her favorites.

Rule #2: Find your angle. Straight, sharp edges, rather than rounded ones, will give a slimming illusion to a round or full face. The highest point of your arch should be just past the outside of your iris.

Rule #3: Call in reinforcements. If your brows are uneven or sparse, use a brow powder or pencil to fill them in. Try to match brow powder or pencil to your hair color as much as possible; go a shade lighter if you need to, rather than darker.

Sculpt cheekbones

"Dark colors create a receding effect," says Laura Geller, owner and creator of Laura Geller Makeup Studio. "By enhancing with a light dusting of powder, any area that is fuller can be changed." Geller, known as "The Queen of Contours," suggest using a sculpting powder – a specially tinted powder whose color mimics the natural shadows of the face. Her lightweight product, Shade-n-Sculpt, features two hues: a soft-brown sculpting shade and a complementary highlighting shade in a soft nude-beige shimmer. Geller offers these pointers:

Geller says a foundation a couple of shades darker than your normal foundation or a bronzer normally used all over your face can be used just to highlight or minimize your problem areas.

Rule #1: Focus on fuller areas. Rather than use contour powder as your cheek color or as an all-over bronzer, Geller advises using it only in specific areas where your bone structure needs to be enhanced, like the cheekbones, jaw, chin and the sides of your nose.

Rule #2: Choose your tools. For the most natural, even results, use a fluffy powder or shading brush "no more than half an inch wide and preferably with natural rather than synthetic bristles," say Gellar. Creating stronger cheekbones? Gently place the powder at the height your cheekbone, almost as far back as the center of your ear.

Rule #3: Blend, baby, blend. Add your blush right on top of the contour powder – a soft stroke will make it look like the contour and blush are one, says Geller. Suck in your lips like a fish, feel for the hollow of your cheekbone, and gently swipe the color right under the bone. Check your reflection in the mirror, and smile!
