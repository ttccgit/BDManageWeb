Why not try a farmer´s market-inspired spa day?

Article By:Bunny Wong

An apple a day may keep not only the doctor away, but the dermatologist, too. That´s because the nutrients that make fruits and vegetables good for your health are also excellent for skin and hair. Take a peek inside your fridge, and you´ll find the ingredients for a bevy of beauty treatments.

Consider these creative masks a budget-wise splurge: They´re a great way to use up fruits and veggies that lurk in your refrigerator´s produce drawer at the end of the week.

No time for homemade? Try these:
  • Body Shop Pink Grapefruit Body Scrub, $17.50. Boost circulation and exfoliate dull skin with this bright, fruity scrub.
  • Burt´s Bees Beeswax & Banana Hand Creme, $8. Potassium-rich bananas are gentle on sensitive skin.
  • Marc Jacobs Splash in Cucumber, $65. The veggie´s astringent qualities imbue this perfume with a summery, refreshing scent.
  • Juice Beauty SPF 15 Tinted Lip Moisturizer in Joyful, $15. This lightly-colored lip balm is made with peach and guava extracts.

Tone skin with apples...
"Apples are well-suited for rough, dry skin," says natural-beauty expert Janice Cox, author of Natural Beauty at Home. Cox explains that the fruit may soften and deep-clean skin without over-drying. Whip up a toner by mixing 1/4 cup no-sugar-added fresh apple juice (fresh pear juice works equally well) and 4 tablespoons witch hazel. Refrigerate in an air-tight container, and apply with a cotton ball.

...then deep-cleanse with a carrot mask.
"Carrots are rich in vitamin A, which is often referred to as the ´beauty´ vitamin because of its importance in maintaining healthy skin," says Cox, who adds that this mask helps skin retain moisture. Mix 2 tablespoons each of fresh carrot juice and white clay (sold at natural-food stores or the health-food section of some supermarkets). Then spread the smooth paste on your face; let rest for 15 minutes. Rinse with warm water, followed by a splash of cold.

Use avocadoes to moisturize hair.
If you´re suffering from dry, frizzy hair, reach for an avocado. Day-spa owner Laura du Priest, author of Natural Beauty: Pamper Yourself with Salon Secrets at Home, recommends a simple, oil-rich blend of one avocado and two tablespoons mayonnaise (or some mashed ripe banana). Massage this concoction through clean, dry hair, and don a shower cap. Let sit for 10 to 20 minutes, and then wash out with warm water and a light shampoo.

Whiten teeth with strawberries.
Rich in salicylic acid, these pretty red berries are a great astringent on their own. If you have oily skin, cut a strawberry in half, rub it gently on your face and rinse; if your skin is more sensitive, mash with a fork, apply for a minute or so and rinse. Strawberries are also incredibly easy-to-use tooth-whiteners. "Just eating a few helps brighten a dull smile," says Cox.

Get beach-ready with pineapples.
These tropical fruits contain be used to cleanse and freshen the skin. Cox´s pineapple body seltzer is great for vacationers with oily skin or acne. Mix 1 cup of fresh pineapple juice (or purée a pineapple in a food processor until smooth) with 2 cups seltzer or sparkling mineral water. Place in a spray bottle and spritz on post-shower.
